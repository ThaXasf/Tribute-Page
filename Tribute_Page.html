Kobe Bryant

The Black Mamba

Here's a time line of Kobe's career:

  • 1996 -Drafted 13th overall
  • 1997 -Wins Slam Dunk Contest
  • 1998 -Participates in first All-Star game
  • 1999 -Becomes an offical starter
  • 2000 -Wins his first NBA Championship
  • 2001 -Wins his second NBA Championship
  • 2002 -Wins his third NBA Championship
  • 2003 -Scores a season high, 55 points, on Michael Jordan's Wizards
  • 2004 -Achieves his third All-NBA First Team and NBA All-Defensive First Team
  • 2005 -Becomes second leading scorer in the NBA season, 27.6 points per game
  • 2006 -Scored career high 81 points in a single game
  • 2007 -Becomes second player in NBA history with four consecutive 50 point games
  • 2008 -Wins his gold medal at the Olympic Games in Beijing
  • 2009 -Wins his fourth NBA Championship
  • 2010 -Wins his fifth NBA Championship
  • 2011 -He is selected for the ninth time, All-NBA First Team and NBA All-Defensive First Team
  • 2012 -Wins his gold medal at the Olympic Games in London
  • 2013 -Becomes first player in NBA history with 47 points, 8 rebounds, 5 assists, 4 blocks, and 3 steals in a single game
  • 2014 -Surpassed Michael Jordan on NBA All-Time Scoring List
  • 2015 -Becomes only player to play 19 seasons and counting with same team that drafted him
  • 2016 -Scored 60 points in his final game

“Kobe’s a model of what a young player should aspire to be. Year by year he learned and made his game more solid, and now he’s not just a highlight-film guy but an accomplished NBA player.”

—Former NBA head coach Larry Brown
